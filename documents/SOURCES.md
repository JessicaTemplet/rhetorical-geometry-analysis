File: DOC-002_treaty_of_versailles_war_guilt_reparations_1919.txt
Contestation: Role of war guilt clause and reparations in destabilizing European governance.
Source: Yale Avalon Project
URL: https://avalon.law.yale.edu/subject_menus/versailles_menu.asp
Notes: Scope limited to: Preamble, Part I (Covenant of the League), Part VII (Penalties,
Articles 227-230), Part VIII (Reparations, Articles 231-247), and the War Guilt clause
(Article 231) specifically. Do not use the full treaty — it runs to hundreds of pages.
Extract only these sections and note the article numbers in the file.

## DOC-005 — Alien and Sedition Acts (1798)
File: DOC-005_alien_and_sedition_acts_1798.txt
Contestation: Tension between national security and the First Amendment.
Source: Yale Avalon Project
URL: https://avalon.law.yale.edu/18th_century/alien.asp
Notes: All four acts should be included in one file:
  - Naturalization Act (June 18, 1798)
  - Alien Friends Act (June 25, 1798)
  - Alien Enemies Act (July 6, 1798)
  - Sedition Act (July 14, 1798)
The Sedition Act is the analytically richest for First Amendment contestation.

## DOC-010 — Emancipation Proclamation (1863)
File: DOC-010_emancipation_proclamation_1863.txt
Contestation: Status as war measure; limited geographic scope at time of issuance;
moral weight vs. legal reach.
Source: National Archives (official transcription)
URL: https://www.archives.gov/exhibits/featured-documents/emancipation-proclamation/transcript.html
Notes: Use the official National Archives transcription. Short document (~700 words).
The geographic carve-outs (excepted parishes and counties) are analytically important —
make sure they are present in the text you use.
Selection note: Selected as tenth document per analyst delegation. Chosen for proposition
density relative to word count, high contestation, and the structural contrast it offers —
a document with limited legal reach that carries enormous moral weight provides a useful
geometric test case.
